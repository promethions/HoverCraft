As you release the air by pulling up the cap, the escaping air creates a hovercraft effect, lifting the CD slightly above the surface - creating an air cushion. The air cushion minimizes friction between the CD and the surface, allowing for smoother movement.

Friction:
Friction is like a stickiness between surfaces, making it harder for things to slide or move smoothly. When a CD is placed on a surface without the balloon, there's more direct contact, and friction slows down its movement. However, when a balloon is attached and air flows out, it creates an air cushion beneath the CD, reducing direct contact and minimizing friction. This cushion allows the CD to hover, gliding smoothly as the escaping air creates a lift. So, the balloon not only adds fun but also reduces friction, making the CD move more easily on the surface.

Newton's third law of motion:
"Every action as an equal and opposite reaction "
As the air rushes out of the balloon, it exerts a force in one direction (downward), causing an equal and opposite reaction force on the CD, lifting it slightly.
